Experimental Data

Access the datasets collected during our Fusion Robotics project, understand their format, and how they are processed.

Open Data for Reproducibility and Research

In the spirit of open source and collaboration, we are sharing the experimental data collected during the development and testing of our Quadruped-UR5 testbed. This data primarily consists of ROS bag files containing timestamped sensor readings and system states recorded during autonomous runs.

Potential uses for this data include:

  • Reproducing our pose estimation comparisons.
  • Testing alternative sensor fusion algorithms.
  • Analyzing quadruped gait performance under different parameters.
  • Developing simulation models based on real-world sensor characteristics.

Data Format and Content

The data is primarily stored as ROS 2 bag files (`.db3` or directory format). You will need a ROS 2 Humble installation to easily inspect and replay these bags using tools like ros2 bag info and ros2 bag play.

Key topics typically included in the bags are:

  • /imu/data (sensor_msgs/Imu): Raw readings from the IMU, including linear acceleration, angular velocity, and raw orientation quaternion (often directly from the sensor's internal filter).
  • /imu/orientation (geometry_msgs/Quaternion): Orientation calculated by the pose_estimator_node after bias removal and gyro integration.
  • /imu/pose_estimate (geometry_msgs/PoseStamped): Full pose (position + orientation) estimated purely from IMU data integration by the pose_estimator_node. Subject to drift.
  • /orb/pose (geometry_msgs/PoseStamped): Full pose estimated from visual features/optical flow by the orb_pose_estimator_node. Less prone to drift over time but potentially noisier or slower.
  • /servo/state (std_msgs/Float64MultiArray): Actual reported positions of the 8 quadruped servos, read back from the ESP32.
  • /servo/command (std_msgs/Float64MultiArray): Commanded positions sent to the servos by the CPGControllerNode.
  • /tf and /tf_static: Coordinate frame transformations used within the system.

Data Filtering and Usage

The raw sensor data undergoes processing within our nodes:

  • IMU Bias Removal: The pose_estimator_node performs an initial calibration to calculate accelerometer and gyroscope biases, which are then subtracted from subsequent raw readings.
  • IMU Filtering: Simple low-pass filtering (using an exponential moving average with `alpha = 0.8`) is applied to the bias-corrected accelerometer and gyroscope data within the pose_estimator_node to reduce noise before integration. Velocity estimates are also decayed over time to mitigate drift.
  • Visual Pose Smoothing: The orb_pose_estimator_node uses a Savitzky-Golay filter (`savgol_filter` with window size 11, polynomial order 2) on a buffer of recent translation estimates to smooth the output visual pose (`/orb/pose`).
  • UI Plotting: The plotter_node subscribes to most of the key topics (IMU data, servo states, both pose estimates) and stores them in a buffer (shared_data). The Flask web application (app.py) serves this buffered data to the web UI for real-time visualization.
  • Pose Estimation: The filtered IMU data is integrated in pose_estimator_node to produce /imu/pose_estimate. Visual features and optical flow are processed in orb_pose_estimator_node to produce /orb/pose. These two estimates can be compared (e.g., visually in the UI) to assess performance and drift characteristics.

Please refer to any accompanying README.md files within the data repository for specific details about individual datasets or recording conditions.
